MEng Thesis: Gas Sensing for Cooling and Combustion using MEMS

Oxford Thermofluids Institute (Oct 2022 to Jun 2023). Supervised by Professor Kam Chana and Professor John Coull.

Skills: MEMS, Electronics, High-pressure gas, Finite-elements MATLAB model, Waterbath calibration, Soldering

During this MEng project, I worked to adapt a conventional gas flow sensor to a gas composition analyser. This industry-oriented project started by modelling the MEMS sensor in MATLAB through a finite-elements approach, and experimentally validating and calibrating the model using argon, air and exhaled air.
